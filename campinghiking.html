Camping & Hiking

I grew up on Lantau on Discovery Bay, this was enjoyable as I was able to really enjoy the outdoors of Hong Kong, and I enjoy both hiking and camping as they both are great ways to experience nature and a different side of Hong Kong. Both activities are best during the winter months however if you’re brave and active like my friends weather will not affect your plans.

Dragons Back

Hong Kong Trail
Section 8, Shek O


This is one of Hong Kong’s most famous hikes, it is relatively easy and only takes an hour to an hour and a half. Dragons-back is not challenging once you get onto the path over the peak, before this there is some serious incline on stairs. The views during this outdoor adventure are spectacular, this is a great hike for all ages and for anyone who wants to get out of the city.

Lo Kei Wan

Shui Hau Village
Lantau, Hong Kong


This campsite is lovely, it is a little out of the way as it takes around half an hour to walk to from the main road and Shui Hau village. The site has a great beach and a decent sized camping area, however it must be said there are no shops or well-maintained facilities. Despite these shortcomings it is a lovely place to camp due to the large amounts of wood for fuel, boundless space and a great environment.

Pui O

Pui O Village
Lantau, Hong Kong


This campsite is very close to Pui O village and only takes a few minutes to walk to from the main road. Though you need to rent out the camping sites it is decently priced and all sites have access to BBQ pits, the beach and public toilets. This is a great place for people who don’t need the privacy and would like to enjoy a relaxing time on the beach.
